COO

Patrick Scott

My name is Patrick D. Scott a long term offender who served 31 years of incarceration. On April 18, 2020 I was released to parole supervision. I participated in multiple educational, self-help and job skill programs during my time in prison. Those programs aided me in decision making and positive critical thinking, I received plenty of support from my wife helping me to be re-acclimated into society. Soon after my release into the community I immediately began to put into motion a plan that I dreamed and designed before I was granted parole. D.O.R.T, H.Y. (Defenders of Real Truth Helping Youth). It took me less than thirty days to find full-time employment with the United Parcel Service in Oakland, Ca. I remain gainfully employed there in the present day. I was determined to become a provider for my household, but more than that I was determined to become a source of inspiration for others. Most of my inspiration comes from my hardships, turmoil, personal struggles, and grief. With that inspiration I created D.O.R.T.H.Y The goal is to bring about a positive change building a support network through unity and investing towards a common cause to reach, teach, and speak to youth offenders or for anyone else who cares to listen. When I'm not working at UPS I'm busy spreading positive messages to the community.

CEO

Melvin Calaway Jr

Founder and CEO of C.C.G Calaway Consultant Group, where he strives to strengthen communities, individuals and organizations through mental health awareness, economic and identity empowerment. Melvin has a proven track record with over ten years of experience as a community service provider, throughout Santa Clara, San Francisco, and Alameda Counties. As a Social worker Melvin is passionate about the work he does for disenfranchised communities, children, and their families. Melvin is a long time native of Oakland, California. As a former a youthful offender, Melvin now provides advocacy and support to returning citizens, showing continuity of care while doing his part in helping to reduce recidivism. Melvin is a registered ASW, working towards his clinical license. Melvin received his Bachelor of Art in psychology from San Francisco State University, master’s in social work from Walden University, where he continues his academic journey as a PhD candidate. He is an experienced public speaker, group facilitator, and mental health consultant.
